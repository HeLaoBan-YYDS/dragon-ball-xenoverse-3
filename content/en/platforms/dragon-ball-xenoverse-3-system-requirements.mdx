## Engine Analysis: Why the System Requirements Are Spiking

The primary reason for the increase in the dragon ball xenoverse 3 system requirements is the game's underlying technology. Dimps is expected to utilize a modern rendering pipeline—likely Unreal Engine 5 or a heavily upgraded proprietary engine—to bring the Dragon Ball universe to life. 

### Advanced Physics and Destructible Environments
During battles, characters crash through mountains, shatter the ground, and vaporize parts of the arena with energy attacks. Processing these real-time physics calculations requires significant CPU power. Older quad-core processors will likely bottleneck your system, causing stuttering during heavy combat sequences.

### Denser Multiplayer Hubs
West City in AGE 1000 is described as a bustling metropolis. Rendering dozens of custom player avatars, NPCs, vehicles, and detailed architecture simultaneously requires substantial system memory (RAM) and video memory (VRAM). If your GPU has less than 6 GB of VRAM, you may experience texture popping and slow loading times when entering populated areas.

### High-Fidelity Particle Effects
Dragon Ball is famous for its flashy energy attacks. Kamehamehas, Final Flashes, and Spirit Bombs create complex lighting and particle effects. Modern graphics cards with dedicated ray-tracing and tensor cores will handle these demanding visual effects far better, allowing for realistic reflections and lighting without tanking your framerate.

Running the game on an older mechanical Hard Disk Drive (HDD) will likely result in severe stuttering, long load times, and audio desynconization.
