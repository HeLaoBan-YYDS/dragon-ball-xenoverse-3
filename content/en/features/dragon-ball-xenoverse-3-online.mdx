## Rebuilding the Foundation: The New Game Engine

One of the most critical revelations from the development team is the decision to move away from the engine used for the first two games. While Xenoverse 1 and 2 shared a core architecture, the third entry is being developed on a completely rebuilt engine. This transition is a double-edged sword for the community; while it promises vastly improved visuals and physics, it also means that porting over the massive roster from the previous game is a significant technical challenge.

According to producer interviews, the team is prioritizing "gameplay mechanics redesign" over simply inflating the character count. The goal is to make every punch, ki blast, and transformation feel more impactful and responsive. Player experience in previous titles often highlighted a "floaty" sensation in combat; the new engine aims to ground the action with better collision detection and environmental destruction.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When is the Dragon Ball Xenoverse 3 release date?
The game is officially scheduled for release in 2027. This allows the developers at Dimps sufficient time to build the new engine from scratch and implement the detailed Age 1000 lore provided by Akira Toriyama’s estate.

### Will my character from Xenoverse 2 carry over to Dragon Ball Xenoverse 3 online?
While the developers haven't confirmed a direct character transfer, they have acknowledged the years of investment players have put into the previous game. However, because the game uses a completely rebuilt engine and new character models, a direct "copy-paste" of avatars is unlikely. There may be "legacy rewards" for returning players instead.

### What platforms will the game be available on?
At launch, the game is confirmed for PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC via Steam. There has been no official word on a Nintendo Switch version, likely due to the high technical demands of the new game engine.

### Is the Dragon Ball Xenoverse 3 online mode a full MMO?
While it isn't a traditional "Massively Multiplayer Online" game, it follows an "MMO-lite" structure. It features a large central hub where players can interact, trade, and form parties, similar to the structure of the previous games but with significantly expanded social and "action performance" features.
