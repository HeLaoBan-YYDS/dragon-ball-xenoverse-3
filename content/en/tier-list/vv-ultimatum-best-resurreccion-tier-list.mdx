## Understanding the Hollow Power Dynamic

Unlike other factions in VV Ultimatum, Hollows rely heavily on the synergy between their chosen Resurrection and their specific skill tree investments. A Resurrection that feels underwhelming on a Strength build might become an absolute powerhouse when paired with Hakuda or Kido. Community reports suggest that the "best" ability is often the one that complements your stat distribution and your ability to "move-stack" during combat.

Move-stacking is a core mechanic where players layer different abilities to create seamless, unavoidable damage windows. For Hollow players, this means looking for Resurrections that offer "true combos"—sequences where the opponent cannot escape or block once the first hit lands. As we dive into the rankings, keep in mind that your personal mechanical skill will always be the most important variable.

## S-Tier: The Apex of the VV Ultimatum Best Resurreccion Tier List

When looking at the top of the food chain, one name consistently rises to the surface: Shark. While it is classified as a Rare rather than a Legendary, its performance in both PVP duels and PVE boss encounters is unmatched.

### Shark (Rare)
Shark is currently widely regarded as the gold standard for Hollow players. Its primary strength lies in its raw damage output and its accessibility. Unlike some higher-tier abilities that require convoluted setups, Shark’s moveset is intuitive and punishing.

*   **Combo Potential:** Shark features moves that transition perfectly into one another, allowing for high-damage sequences that are difficult for opponents to interrupt.
*   **The Variant Factor:** Player experience suggests that the Shark variant provides a significant boost to overall DPS, making it a terrifying prospect in the arena.
*   **Versatility:** It works across various skill-tree combinations, meaning you aren't pigeonholed into a single build path. Whether you are focusing on raw power or a more balanced approach, Shark delivers consistent results.

## A-Tier: High-Skill and Specialized Dominance

The A-Tier consists of abilities that are arguably just as strong as S-Tier options but require more specific builds or a higher level of player skill to unlock their full potential. If you are looking for something more specialized than the all-around power of Shark, these are your best bets.

### Fury (Rare)
Fury is a favorite among veteran players who enjoy the Hakuda (hand-to-hand) playstyle. It is a highly skill-dependent moveset that rewards precision and timing.

*   **Hakuda Synergy:** When paired with the Hakuda tree, Fury becomes a combo machine. The "Elastic" variant is particularly noted for its ability to pull enemies in, setting up "true combos" that lead directly into the devastating Dragon Uppercut.
*   **Mobility:** It offers excellent dash-grab mechanics that allow you to dictate the pace of the fight.
*   **Learning Curve:** Beginners may find Fury difficult to use initially, as missing a grab can leave you wide open to a counter-attack.

### Los Lobos (Legendary)
As one of the few Legendary options, Los Lobos brings a unique flavor to the Hollow experience. It is the premier choice for players who prefer a Kido-heavy, ranged approach.

*   **Ranged Superiority:** Los Lobos excels at kiting—the act of staying out of an enemy's reach while constantly dealing damage. With various firing modes and the massive "Cero Metralleta" nukes, you can melt an opponent's health bar from across the map.
*   **Air Combat:** It features a light attack follow-up that acts as an air launcher, providing excellent verticality and combo extensions in the sky.
*   **Build Restriction:** The main drawback of Los Lobos is its lack of flexibility. It does not pair well with Hakuda or pure Strength builds, making it a dedicated choice for Kido enthusiasts.

## B-Tier: Reliable but Situational

The B-Tier is home to abilities that have clear strengths but also glaring weaknesses that prevent them from reaching the top of the **VV Ultimatum best Resurreccion tier list**.

### Frozen Haze (Common)
Don't let the "Common" tag fool you; Frozen Haze can be surprisingly effective in the right hands. Its power is almost entirely concentrated in its utility moves.

*   **The Mute Mechanic:** The standout feature here is the AOE (Area of Effect) mute move. This ability can temporarily disable an opponent's powers, creating a window where they are essentially defenseless.
*   **Dependency:** The downside is that Frozen Haze relies too heavily on this one gimmick. If you miss your mute or the opponent manages to dodge it, your damage potential drops significantly compared to Shark or Fury.
*   **Upgrades:** To make Frozen Haze viable in late-game scenarios, you must invest heavily in upgrades to bolster its baseline stats.

## C-Tier: The Entry-Level Options

Abilities in the C-Tier are generally outclassed by those above them. While they can get you through the early stages of the game, you will likely want to reroll them as soon as possible for something more competitive.

### Electro (Uncommon)
Electro suffers from a lack of identity in the current meta. It provides decent mobility and some lightning-themed attacks, but it lacks the "oomph" required for high-level play.

*   **Low Damage Floor:** Compared to the Rare and Legendary options, Electro’s damage scaling is lackluster.
*   **Cosmetic Variants:** Unlike Shark or Ice (for Shinigami), the variants for Electro are largely cosmetic and do not offer the mechanical advantages needed to elevate its standing.
*   **Combo Gaps:** There are many "gaps" in Electro's combos where an experienced player can easily escape or use a "reversal" move.

## Pro Tips for Maximizing Your Resurrection

Simply having a top-tier Resurrection isn't enough; you need to build around it. Here are some actionable tips to help you dominate:

1.  **Match Your Traits:** Always look for Hollow Traits that complement your Resurrection. If you are using Los Lobos, prioritize traits that boost Reiatsu or Kido damage. If you are a Shark main, look for physical damage buffs.
2.  **Master the "M2" Follow-up:** Many Resurrections have unique M2 (heavy attack) properties. Learning the exact range and startup frames of your heavy attack is crucial for extending combos after a successful guard break.
3.  **Variant Hunting:** Don't settle for the base version of an ability. Variants like "Red Ice" for Shinigami or "Elastic" for Fury change the properties of your moves, often adding stun duration or pull-in effects that make your combos much harder to drop.
4.  **Practice in Humanoid PVE:** Before jumping into ranked PVP, practice your move-stacking on humanoid PVE enemies. Their AI often mimics player movement, allowing you to refine your timing without the pressure of losing rank.

## FAQ: VV Ultimatum Best Resurreccion Tier List

### What is currently the best Resurrection for beginners?
For players just starting their journey as a Hollow, **Shark** is widely considered the best choice. It is a Rare ability, meaning it is easier to obtain than a Legendary like Los Lobos, yet it offers some of the highest and most consistent damage in the game. Its combos are straightforward, making it very forgiving for those still learning the mechanics of move-stacking.

### Is rarity the most important factor in the VV Ultimatum best Resurreccion tier list?
No, rarity is not the end-all-be-all. As seen in our rankings, the Uncommon "Ice" Shikai and the Rare "Shark" Resurrection often outperform Legendary options in pure PVP duels. While Legendaries like Los Lobos or Superstar offer unique "modes" and flashy effects, many Rares and Uncommons have better "true combo" potential and utility in a 1v1 scenario.

### How do variants affect the ranking of a Resurrection?
Variants can drastically change an ability's position on the **VV Ultimatum best Resurreccion tier list**. A variant can add status effects (like the freeze duration on Red Ice) or change the physical properties of a move (like the pull-in effect on the Elastic variant of Fury). These changes often turn a "good" ability into an "overpowered" one by enabling combos that were previously impossible.

### Can I still win with a C-Tier Resurrection like Electro?
While it is possible to win with any ability if your mechanical skill is significantly higher than your opponent's, you will be at a distinct disadvantage. C-Tier abilities generally have lower damage scaling, easier-to-dodge moves, and fewer combo opportunities. If you are serious about competitive PVP, it is highly recommended to use your rerolls to aim for at least a B-Tier or A-Tier option.
