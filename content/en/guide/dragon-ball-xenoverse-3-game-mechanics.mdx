## Official Leaks: Soul Assist, Soul Change, and V Jump Revelations

The latest V Jump scans have unveiled two major additions to the combat system: "Soul Assist" and "Soul Change." These features represent a significant departure from the static ally systems of previous games. Instead of simply summoning a companion to throw a single blast, these mechanics integrate your roster into active combat.

Soul Assist allows benched characters to provide passive buffs or execute coordinated follow-up attacks during specific combo strings. This adds a layer of deck-building strategy to your character selection. On the other hand, Soul Change functions as a dynamic tag system. Players can swap their active fighter mid-combo to extend pressure or escape a disadvantageous position. 

| Mechanic Name | Primary Function | Tactical Advantage | Trigger Condition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Soul Assist** | Passive stat boosts and automatic combo extensions | Increases damage output without interrupting flow | Reaching specific combo thresholds |
| **Soul Change** | Active character swap during live combat | Escapes enemy pressure and extends custom combos | Consumes a portion of the stamina/utility bar |
| **Traditional Assist** | Summoning an ally for a single, pre-determined attack | Distracts opponents or covers space | Manual button input with a cooldown |

These additions indicate that team synergy will play a much larger role in competitive matches. Players will need to balance their custom Time Patrollers with cast characters who offer the best mechanical synergy.

## Combat System Evolution: Clashes, Wall Crashes, and Ki Stuns

Delving into the core dragon ball xenoverse 3 game mechanics showcased in recent media, we see a heavy influence from other popular Dragon Ball titles. Community reports indicate that developers are looking at successful elements from games like *Sparking! ZERO* and the classic *Raging Blast* series to enhance the moment-to-moment combat.

One of the most requested features is the inclusion of dedicated clash mechanics. In previous entries, simultaneous attacks often resulted in awkward trade animations. The new system introduces rush clashes, ki blast clashes, and grab clashes that require rapid inputs or precise timing to win.

Additionally, the "Wall Crash" mechanic from *Raging Blast* is making a return. When a character is knocked into a boundary, they become temporarily pinned. To prevent this from becoming overpowered, developers have implemented a damage-scaling system. While pinned, opponents take standard damage from melee strikes, but heavy energy beams deal reduced damage to allow for a quick recovery.

| Combat Maneuver | Inspiration Source | Mechanical Execution | Balancing Measure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rush Clash** | *Sparking! ZERO* | Rapid button pressing during simultaneous dashes | High risk, high reward; drains stamina of the loser |
| **Wall Crash** | *Raging Blast* | Knocking an opponent into physical arena boundaries | Pinned targets take reduced damage from Ultimate attacks |
| **Swing Dodge** | *Raging Blast* | Manual directional slip to evade close-range strikes | Requires precise timing; vulnerable to guard breaks |
| **Key Stun (Nerfed)** | *Xenoverse 2* | Low-priority projectile used to interrupt movement | Priority rate lowered; cannot interrupt heavy animations |

The controversial "Ki Stun" tactic from the previous game is also receiving a major overhaul. Player experience reports suggest that the priority rate of basic Ki blasts has been adjusted. In the new game, these minor projectiles will no longer override major attack animations, leveling the playing field for defensive playstyles.

## Visual Realism: Permanent Stage Destruction and Physics

A common criticism of older arena fighters is the lack of lasting environmental impact. In Xenoverse 2, massive craters created by planet-destroying attacks would vanish after a few seconds. The new engine address this issue by introducing permanent stage destruction.

When you deflect a massive energy wave or slam an opponent into the ground, the resulting damage remains visible for the duration of the match. The battlefield slowly transforms into a war zone, reflecting the intensity of the fight. This visual storytelling is paired with upgraded character physics. 

Clothing and hair now react dynamically to power-ups. When a character charges their Ki, their clothing flaps violently, and their hair sways in response to the energy current. Furthermore, dynamic clothing destruction has been upgraded. Instead of simple texture swaps, outfits tear and shred realistically based on the types of attacks received.

| Feature | Xenoverse 2 Standard | Xenoverse 3 Upgrade | Impact on Gameplay |
