## A Brand-New Engine: Why Xenoverse 3 is a True Sequel

One of the most significant revelations from the developers is that the game does not reuse the foundation of the first two entries. Executive producer Masayuki Hirano confirmed that the team completely rebuilt the game engine for this sequel. This represents a major departure from the past, where the first and second games shared the same core technology.

Rebuilding the engine allows for more dynamic destruction, smoother animations, and modern netcode. However, it also presents a massive development challenge. The team cannot simply port over the massive roster of characters from the previous game without rebuilding their assets and animations.

With a completely rebuilt engine, the dragon ball xenoverse 3 price reflects a ground-up development process rather than a simple asset reuse. The developers are redesigning core gameplay mechanics to ensure combat feels responsive, fresh, and distinct from the decade-old systems of the past.

## Exploring Age 1000: The Lore and Akira Toriyama's Legacy

Instead of forcing players to play through the Saiyan, Namek, and Cell sagas yet again, the sequel is moving far into the future. The game takes place in Age 1000, an era previously touched upon in the lore of *Dragon Ball Online*. This future setting features a highly advanced, technology-filled corner of the universe.

In Age 1000, players will explore a fully realized West City. The story involves joining the ranks of the Great Saiyan Squad, working alongside new allies to solve emerging threats. This setting gives the developers the creative freedom to introduce new characters and redesign familiar faces in logical ways.

Importantly, this setting is heavily rooted in the late Akira Toriyama's personal notes and design documents. The development team expressed experiencing a deep sense of responsibility to honor his legacy.
