## Character Creation (CAC) Overhaul: Hair, Muscles, and Outfits

When building a comprehensive **dragon ball xenoverse 3 wishlist**, the first area players point to is the outdated Character Creation (CAC) system. Xenoverse 2 relied heavily on static assets, leaving custom characters looking remarkably similar to one another.

### Transforming Hairstyles and Deeper Customization

Player experiences on forums and social media highlight a massive grievance: static Super Saiyan hair. In the current engine, going Super Saiyan simply turns a character's hair yellow without changing its shape. Community reports frequently praise PC mods like the "Revamp Project" for proving that transforming hairstyles are entirely possible. 

We need custom hair that actually spikes up or changes form when transforming. Additionally, the game must move away from using poorly scaled wigs as accessories and instead offer genuine, high-quality hairstyles for all races, including unique head shapes for the Frieza race and diverse options for female Majins.

| Customization Feature | Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2 Status | Wishlist Target for Xenoverse 3 |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Super Saiyan Hair** | Color change only (static yellow) | Fully animated, spiking transformation models |
| **Hairstyle Variety** | Extremely limited; unchanged for years | Ported roster styles, mod-inspired creations |
| **Wigs vs. Hair** | Wigs take up valuable accessory slots | Wigs converted into select, editable hairstyles |
| **Eyebrow Syncing** | Fixed color; requires premium stylist | Dynamic color matching based on active transformations |

### Dynamic Body Styles and Muscle Definition

Currently, adjusting a CAC's physical build simply stretches or thins the character model. This results in awkward proportions rather than realistic muscle mass. 

To fix this, Dimps should introduce a dedicated muscle slider. This would allow players to design bulky, powerhouse brawlers or lean, high-speed martial artists, closely mirroring the clear physical differences seen between early Dragon Ball Z characters and their late-series counterparts.

### Casual Clothes and Animated Accessories

While the franchise is deeply rooted in martial arts, community feedback indicates a strong desire for more casual clothing options. Outfits like Trunks’ classic hoodie or custom streetwear would allow players to express themselves in the hub world. 

Another major pillar of any **dragon ball xenoverse 3 wishlist** is the implementation of fully animated Saiyan tails. In Xenoverse 2, equipped tails are completely static and stiff. Players want to see dynamic, swaying tails that react to movement, combat, and transformations.

The current race selection—Saiyan, Earthling, Majin, Namekian, and Frieza Race—laid a solid foundation. However, a true sequel must push these boundaries further to keep the gameplay fresh.

For example, choosing an Earthling could lead to a cybernetic "Android" branch or a three-eyed clan pathway. ## Gameplay Mechanics: Custom Mentor Movesets and Cinematic Awoken Skills

Combat in Xenoverse has always been flashy, but it often lacks structural variety. Introducing deeper mechanical customization would elevate the competitive and casual scenes alike.

### Mixing and Matching Combos

A highly requested feature in player circles is the ability to adopt a mentor’s fighting style. If your CAC is trained by Gohan, you should be able to equip Gohan’s light and heavy combo strings. 

Taking it a step further, allowing players to mix and match combo strings—such as utilizing Vegito's rapid kicks for light attacks and MUI Goku's strikes for heavy attacks—would provide unparalleled combat variety.

### True Awoken Forms for Non-Saiyans

Saiyans have historically received the lion's share of transformations. To balance this, the next title needs to introduce high-tier Awoken skills for other races. 

Namekians deserve the "Orange Namekian" or "Power Awakening" forms, while the Frieza race should be able to transition through their second, third, and final forms rather than jumping straight to Golden.

| Race | Proposed Awoken Skill | Visual and Gameplay Changes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Namekian** | Orange / Power Awakened | Massive size increase, orange skin tone, heavy defense buff |
| **Frieza Race** | Multi-Form Progression | Cycle through Form 2, Form 3, and Final Form mid-battle |
| **Majin** | Absorption / Super Form | Absorb opponent's traits or transition into a sleek Super Buu model |
| **Earthling** | Weapon Mastery / Buff Form | Utilize Master Roshi-style bulk or advanced physical weaponry |

## A Reimagined Hub World and Campaign

Conton City was a massive step up from Tokitoki City, but it eventually became empty and static. The next hub world needs to feel alive, interactive, and seamless.

### Merging Conton City and Tokitoki City

A popular community idea is to merge the historical landmarks of Tokitoki City with the sprawling landscapes of Conton City. This new hub should feature dynamic weather, day-night cycles, and interactive mini-games. 

Furthermore, players should be able to fly anywhere instantly—including inside the Time Nest—without dealing with slow walking speeds or arbitrary invisible walls.

### A Cinematic, Rebooted Story

With the official depiction of the Demon Realm expanding across modern Dragon Ball media, the story of Xenoverse needs a complete reboot. Instead of simply replaying the Saiyan Saga for the third time with minor historical changes, the narrative should dive deep into unexplored "What-If" scenarios. 

Imagine a campaign where Gohan Black exists, or where players must stop a fused Beerus and Whis. Giving players choices that branch the story would make the Time Patrol feel like an actual choice-driven adventure.
