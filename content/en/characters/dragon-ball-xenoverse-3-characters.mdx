## Soul Bonding & Soul Exchange: A Revolution in Combat

One of the most ground-breaking features introduced in this sequel is the concept of Soul Bonding, which draws inspiration from mechanics seen in *Dragon Ball FighterZ* and *The Breakers*. This system allows your custom character (CaC) to form a deep spiritual connection with legendary Z Warriors. During combat, this bond manifests in two distinct ways: Soul Exchange and Soul Assist. 

Through Soul Exchange, players can literally channel the soul of an iconic hero directly into their custom character. When activated, a stunning cutscene plays where the hero's soul merges with your avatar. For a limited time (initially set to 41 seconds in early gameplay builds), you take full control of that character, gaining complete access to their physical combat style, supers, and ultimate abilities. While the Soul Exchange is active, your character's maximum health increases dramatically, and a unique Capsule Corp-styled halo appears above their head.

Example: A human custom character named Sen has her health boosted from 60,900 HP to 79,200 HP immediately upon exchanging souls with Future Trunks.

## Custom Characters (CaCs) and Fighting Style Sharing

One of the most requested features in the history of the franchise has finally been implemented. In this sequel, custom characters are no longer locked into generic, race-based basic attack strings. Instead, the game introduces Fighting Style Sharing, which allows you to borrow the movesets and combat styles of iconic **dragon ball xenoverse 3 characters**.

If you prefer the elegant, sword-based combat of Future Trunks, you can assign his exact melee flow to your Earthling, Saiyan, or Namekian custom character. If you prefer the aggressive, close-quarters rushdowns of Vegeta or the highly calculated combat flow of Ultimate Gohan, you can master their styles and equip them directly to your avatar. 

Unlike previous games where custom avatars had restricted movesets, the list of **dragon ball xenoverse 3 characters** serves as a direct training ground. This feature functions similarly to specialized sports game builds, giving players complete freedom to mix and match their favorite fighter's physical animations with their custom character's unique aesthetic.

| Step | Action | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Bond with a Mentor** | Interact with legendary characters in New City to unlock their combat styles. |
| **2** | **Master the Flow** | Complete combat trials and training missions to fully unlock their melee animations. |
| **3** | **Equip the Style** | Go to the customization menu and apply the unlocked fighting style to your CaC. |
| **4** | **Combine with Supers** | Pair your newly acquired melee animations with any compatible super attacks. |

## Enhanced Transformations and Visual Upgrades

Transformations have received a massive overhaul, addressing a long-standing critique from the community. In previous titles, transforming into a Super Saiyan would change your character's hair color, but the actual 3D model of the hair remained identical to its base form. 

This game introduces dynamic hair rendering for transformations. When your custom character transforms into a Super Saiyan, their hair physically spikes up and shifts into an entirely unique, aggressive style. For advanced forms like Super Saiyan 3, the game features highly cinematic, real-time transition animations. The camera zooms in, pans behind the character's head, and shows the hair physically growing longer in realistic, staggered stages until it reaches its full, iconic length.

## Cooperative Combat and Boss Mechanics

The multiplayer experience has been heavily upgraded to emphasize team coordination and strategic planning. The combat system incorporates MMO-lite mechanics, allowing players to coordinate their offenses to lock down powerful raid bosses like Broly. 

For instance, players can coordinate their attacks to specifically target and drain an enemy's Ki gauge. Successfully draining this gauge prevents the boss from executing high-damage ultimate attacks, effectively neutralizing their offensive capabilities. Additionally, if a teammate is knocked down in battle, the entire surviving team can rush to their aid. Having multiple players participate in a revive significantly speeds up the recovery animation, getting your ally back into the fight almost instantly.

According to player experiences and early community reports, the game also features a highly amusing yet practical combat behavior: while an enemy boss is locked in an animation manhandling or ragdolling one of your teammates, the other players can continue to deal massive damage to the boss from a distance. This means getting targeted by a boss can actually serve as a tactical decoy, allowing your squad to dogpile the enemy with physical strikes and Ki blasts while their guard is down.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Who are the confirmed dragon ball xenoverse 3 characters so far?
The initial roster of confirmed **dragon ball xenoverse 3 characters** features a mix of classic fighters and completely original heroes. Playable characters include the custom male and female Time Patrollers and Future Trunks. Notable NPCs include Bulma (Age 1000), Brett (Saiya Brett), Gamma 1, Rom, Lilica, Tap, Shenron, and Saiyan Saga Vegeta. ### How does the Soul Exchange mechanic work in battle?
Soul Exchange allows your custom character to merge with the soul of an iconic hero like Future Trunks. When activated, you take full control of that hero's moveset, super attacks, and physical combat style for a limited time (initially 41 seconds). This transformation also grants a massive temporary boost to your maximum health pool and displays a unique Capsule Corp halo above your character's head.

### Can my custom character use the fighting style of iconic characters?
Yes! The game features a Fighting Style Sharing system. By bonding with and training under various legendary characters throughout New City, your custom character can learn and equip their exact basic melee attack strings and animations. This allows you to fight using Trunks' sword style, Vegeta's aggressive rushdown, or Gohan's tactical combat flow, regardless of your character's chosen race.

The game is currently scheduled for a Japanese release in 2027. It is being developed by Dimps and published by Bandai Namco. The confirmed platforms for the game are the PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X/S, and Microsoft Windows via Steam.
