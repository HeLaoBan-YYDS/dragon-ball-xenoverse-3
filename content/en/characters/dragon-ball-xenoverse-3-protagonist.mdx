## Rebuilding the Foundation: A New Engine for a New Hero

One of the most significant revelations from series producer Masayuki Hirano is that the game is not being built on the foundation of its predecessors. While Xenoverse 1 and 2 shared a core engine, the third game features a completely rebuilt engine. This decision was made to ensure the game feels like a true leap forward rather than just "Xenoverse 2 with a new coat of paint."

For the **dragon ball xenoverse 3 protagonist**, this means more than just better graphics. A rebuilt engine allows for redesigned gameplay mechanics, smoother animations, and a more responsive combat system. However, this shift comes with its own set of challenges. Hirano-san noted that bringing over the massive roster of characters from previous games will be difficult because every asset has to be reworked for the new technical framework.

Community reports and player experiences from previous transitions suggest that fans are often wary of "roster cuts." However, the trade-off for a more robust engine is a protagonist that moves, fights, and interacts with the environment in ways that simply weren't possible on the old hardware. The focus is on quality and depth over sheer quantity, ensuring that every transformation and special move feels impactful.

This shift acknowledges that the Xenoverse community loves the "weirdness" of Dragon Ball—the moments where players gather in the hub world to perform synchronized poses or show off their unique fashion sense.

The goal is to make the player feel like they are "existing" in a shared Dragon Ball space, rather than just moving from mission to mission. This level of expression ensures that your protagonist isn't just a generic fighter, but a character with a distinct "vibe" that reflects your personal style.
